Section 01

Pipeline Overview

Raw OHLCV data flows through six transformation layers before reaching the LightGBM LambdaRank model. Each layer adds dimensionless, cross-sectionally comparable signals.

ⓘ ATR normalization — almost every distance and slope feature is divided by Wilder's 14-day ATR, making signals dimensionless and comparable across stocks at ₹100 vs ₹10,000.
Section 02

Volatility Features

ATR is the universal ruler. Every distance, slope, and return feature is expressed in ATR units so signals are comparable across the full universe regardless of price or sector.

True Range — the 3-component measurement

Wilder's ATR uses the largest of three possible ranges each day, capturing gap moves that a simple High−Low misses.

atr_pct_rank_252 = rank_pct( ATR_t within rolling 252-day window ) — low rank ≈ pre-breakout coil (calm before storm) vol_contraction = ATR_t / max(ATR over last 60 days) → range (0, 1] compression_score = 1 − vol_contraction → high = squeezed

Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)

vol_contraction detects the classic squeeze before a breakout — ATR shrinks relative to its 60-day peak. Lower = more compressed = higher compression_score.

High vol_contraction (≈ 1.0)

ATR at 60-day peak — explosive move or trend start. Wide daily ranges.

Low vol_contraction (< 0.25)

ATR compressed — textbook VCP / Bollinger squeeze. High compression_score → setup pending.

Section 03

Trend & Momentum Features

ADX measures trend strength (not direction). SMA slopes confirm direction. Return features capture momentum persistence across four timeframes.

ADX — Trend Strength Gauge

0–100 range, direction-agnostic. Low ADX = sideways/choppy; high ADX = strong trend.

Multi-Timeframe Trend Stack

Four rolling SMA thresholds define whether each timeframe is "up" or "down". All four green = institutional confirmation.

Section 03b

SMA-Based & 52-Week Range Features

Price position relative to the three key moving averages tells the model "where in its trend lifecycle" a stock sits. The 52-week range position acts as the primary universe filter that splits stocks into momentum vs reversal mode.

Price vs SMA20 / SMA50 / SMA200

Each distance is expressed in ATR units: (Close − SMA_N) / ATR_14. Positive = above SMA (bullish). A stock above SMA200 with rising SMA200 slope is a Minervini Stage 2 setup — the model weights this heavily.

price_vs_sma200 > 0

Stock above 200-day SMA = Mark Minervini Stage 2 bull market. The LGBM model treats this as the strongest long-term bullish gate. Combined with sma200_slope_10 > 0 (rising), this is maximum institutional alignment.

Buy-the-dip pattern

Stock far above SMA200 + recently pulling back to SMA50 = textbook momentum continuation. The model recognizes the SMA50 as the "institutional defence level" — where funds add to positions.

52-Week Range Position — Universe Filter

high_52w_dist is the single most important feature for mode selection. It determines which model ranks a stock.

high_52w_dist_t = (Close_t − rolling_252_High_t) / rolling_252_High_t ≤ 0 low_52w_dist_t = (Close_t − rolling_252_Low_t) / rolling_252_Low_t ≥ 0 Momentum universe : high_52w_dist > −0.15 (within 15% of 52w high) Reversal universe : high_52w_dist ≤ −0.20 (more than 20% below 52w high)
Section 03c

Volume Features & Market Regime

Volume confirms price action — high volume breakouts are real; low volume moves fade. The market regime (Bull/Choppy/Bear) gates how much weight all other features carry.

Volume Ratio Features

Two volume features capture different time horizons: vol_ratio_5d (today vs 20d avg) catches breakout spikes; vol_ratio_20d (5d avg vs 60d avg) catches sustained accumulation/distribution shifts.

vol_ratio_5d > 2.0

Today's volume is 2× the 20-day average. Distinguishes real breakouts from low-volume drifts. Combines with ICT signals for highest-confidence entries.

vol_ratio_20d — Sustained Shift

5-day avg vs 60-day avg. Smooths one-day spikes — catches sustained institutional accumulation over a week, not just an intraday surge.

Market Regime — Bull / Choppy / Bear

Three mutually-exclusive dummy variables derived from the NIFTY benchmark. The regime is a context gate — the same stock setup is far more reliable in a bull regime than a choppy or bear one.

ⓘ The regime features act as a macro context gate inside the LightGBM tree — the model can branch differently based on regime before evaluating stock-specific signals. A strong demand zone in a bear regime is far less reliable than the same zone in a bull regime.
Section 04

ICT Features — Order Blocks, Breaker Blocks, FVGs

Inner Circle Trader (ICT) signals identify institutional accumulation/distribution footprints. The pipeline runs detection on 5 timeframes (daily → yearly) and produces a weighted HTF composite score.

Order Block (OB) — Bullish & Bearish

The last opposite-color candle before a strong breakout. Institutions "load" their orders here, then drive price away. When price returns to the zone, those resting orders re-activate.

ict_bob_dist_t = (Close_t − zone_midpoint) / ATR_14_t × session_weight positive when price is above zone midpoint (approaching from below)

Breaker Block (BB) — Highest Priority ICT Signal

A failed OB that flips polarity after a liquidity sweep. The reclaim above an OB body — after stop-hunting retail traders — is the highest-conviction ICT signal (priority 3, vs OB=2, FVG=1).

Fair Value Gap (FVG) — 3-Candle Imbalance

When the middle candle moves so fast that there is a gap between candle 1's high and candle 3's low. Price "wants to fill" this inefficiency. Invalidated when price crosses the gap midpoint (tighter than OB/BB).

Liquidity Sweep — BSL & SSL Stop Hunts

Retail stop-losses cluster just beyond equal highs/lows. Institutional orders engineer a wick to grab those stops before reversing — the hallmark of smart money distribution/accumulation.

Section 05

Supply / Demand Zones — DZ, SZ, SDZ, SSZ

The most important features for the bull/bear composite (30–45% of final ranking score). Zones are drawn from base candle patterns and represent where institutional resting orders are waiting.

The 4 Base Candle Patterns

A zone requires a small base candle sandwiched between two larger candles. The surrounding candle direction determines whether it is supply or demand.

SDZ — Swap Demand Zone (Strongest Bullish Signal)

A former Supply Zone that price cleanly broke above. Old institutional resistance flips to support. The model weights SDZ on the yearly timeframe at 3.5× — the single heaviest feature in the bull composite.

SSZ — Swap Supply Zone (Strongest Bearish Signal)

The exact bear-side mirror of SDZ. A former Demand Zone (DZ) that price cleanly broke below — old institutional support has been overwhelmed and flips to overhead resistance on any retest. ssz_htf_score drives the bear composite the same way sdz_htf_score drives the bull composite.

Section 06

Composite Scoring & Final Pipeline

The final rank blends 90% LGBM + 10% inverse-vol, then mixes the model score with the zone composite at 70/30 (momentum) or 55/45 (reversal).

Zone Timeframe Weights

Higher timeframes carry exponentially more weight — a yearly SDZ is worth 5× a daily SDZ. The SDZ/SSZ multiplier (×2) makes swap zones worth twice as much as standard DZ/SZ.

sdz_raw_score = Σ(weight_tf × 2 × is_SDZ_tf) / 30 → range [0, 1] dz_raw_score = Σ(weight_tf × 1 × is_DZ_tf) / 30 → range [0, 1] sdz_htf_score = sdz_raw_score × up_mult where up_mult = 0.5 + 0.375×(wkly + mnthly + qtly + yrly) range [0.5 no trends] → [2.0 all trends up]

sdz_htf_score — Zone × Trend Confluence

Multiplying the zone score by trend alignment means a yearly SDZ in a confirmed uptrend scores dramatically higher than the same zone in a downtrend.

Final Scoring Pipeline

Two parallel streams — ML model and zone composite — are weighted and blended. Reversal mode shifts weight toward zones because price action alone is insufficient for catching falling knives.

ModeUniverse filterModel weightComposite weightRationale
Momentum high_52w_dist > −0.15 70% 30% Price action already strong — zones add confirmation
Reversal high_52w_dist ≤ −0.20 55% 45% Need strong demand zone to justify catching a falling knife
⚠ The zone composite is intentionally narrow — only SDZ/SSZ signals. Everything else (ICT, trend, momentum, volume, regime, SMA) is owned entirely by the ML model, which learns those weights from data. Zone signals are rare, rule-based, and hard-coded — exactly why they belong in the composite.
